Bootcamp Project - Music Store: Piranha

Detailed web design (UX/UI).

About this project

Goal

This is a real store, that sent a briefing to the school, so that we design students could use it as a project. It is a store that sells CD's, Vinyls and collectables of the music industry, new or in second hand.
Their current website is this, and it needs a change.
The point was to make the students analyze the briefing, the existing problems and the possible solutions.

Problem

Their website is in a bad shape and targets a specific audience, when the store wants a more diverse one.
The store also wants to have a stronger online presence and sell internationally but their website is not able to achieve that.

Solution

Make a more modern design, that welcomes a more diverse type of customer but not changing the roots of the store.
The store would have new sections such as returns and second hand items, and it would also have new functionalities such as PT and EN as options to use for the language.

My duties and tools

- Wireframes and design style guide, in Figma
- Site-map (in group), in Figma
- Moodboard (in group), in Figma
- Project's tasks management (in group), in Miro

My influence

I decided to add the new sections in the website, alongside the PT-EN option,since the store wants to focus more on online sales and also internationally.
Most of the customers are not really into computers, so they don't understand much of UI, plus most of the customers are 30 plus years old and because of that, I wanted the design to be very simple, with very obvious buttons and icons to make the user flow a lot friendly with a lot of white space.

Difficulties/What I learned

Unlike the bakery, this project has an existing store and identity. This project shifted my mindset from creativeness to analyizing the market, what is failing and why, reading user reviews online and try to find the solutino to those real existing problems. I understood that design is how it works, and not only how it looks.
