Bootcamp Project - Bakery

Web design (UX/UI) and logo design (branding).

About this project

Goal

This fictional bakery is a high end bakery, specialized in muffins, which has a goal of being known as the most elegant and prestige bakery in Porto.
It has a very minimalistic and calm identity, and believes that having one of their muffins is not eating food, is having an experience.

Problem

Bakery had no online presence and no way to claim the prestige it was desired to have.
It needed a way to make sure people understood right away, without reading, without thinking much, that the product would be delicious, an experience, a moment, expensive but worth it.

Solution

To solve that I made a friendly user-flow, a minimalistic design with luxury associated colors, with very little amount of texts in the website. Images would be the main form of interaction in the website, because it is prestige, the product sells itself alongside the design.

My duties

- Create user personas
- Did user research on fictional customers
- Researched the market and potential competitors
- Created a site map
- Designed lo-fi and hi-fi wireframes
- Create a design style guide
- Create a logo

Tools used

- Wireframes were made in Adobe XD
- Pictures were edited in Adobe Photoshop
- Necessary details were edited in Adobe Illustrator
- Project was organized in Miro

Difficulties / What I learned

Since it was a bootcamp project of a fictional bakery, there are no real users, no user research, so I tried to imagine what the pain, needs, motivations and fears of an ideal or possible customer and how they would relate to the product. I then designed based on that information for their best possible experience using the product.
